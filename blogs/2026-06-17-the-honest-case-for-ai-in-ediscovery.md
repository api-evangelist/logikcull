The article examines where AI genuinely delivers value in eDiscovery versus where marketing hype oversells its capabilities, arguing that AI delivers measurable value in a few specific places and oversells itself in others. It contends AI is most useful for high-volume, pattern-based work like document prioritization and initial tagging, but falls short in areas requiring legal judgment such as privilege determinations and case strategy.
